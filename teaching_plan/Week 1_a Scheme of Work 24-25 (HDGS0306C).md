COURSE AIMS:

This course aims to prepare students for tertiary studies by improving their academic writing skills, with a particular focus on referencing skills, writing structure, tone and style, elements of writing and writing models. Students will learn to find and evaluate reference sources, summarise and paraphrase borrowed material, and apply specified referencing styles to their assignments. They will develop their ability to write sentences, paragraphs and essays, use different language skills to express ideas, and emphasise and control the strength of their claims in their writing. They will practice writing for different purposes such as to argue, discuss, compare and contrast and define and generalise. Students will become familiar with some basic academic writing models such as case studies, literature reviews and reports.



COURSE INTENDED LEARNING OUTCOMES (CILOS)



CILO

By the end of the course, students should be able to:

CILO 1

Deploy learnt skills to construct academic writing at the sentence, paragraph and essay levels;

CILO 2

Compose academic writing using appropriate language, tone and style;

CILO 3

Apply appropriate reference systems, writing elements and writing models to assignments.






TEACHING AND LEARNING ACTIVITIES (TLAs)



CILOs 

TLAs will include the following:

CILOs 1, 2

Students will engage in group discussions to examine, analyse and critique extracts from academic papers. They will identify problems and edit texts to improve their structure and language. They will acquire and develop self-editing strategies specific to academic writing.

CILOs 1, 2

Students will apply academic writing techniques and compose paragraphs. They will engage in peer reviews of the assignments to enhance sensitivity to common mistakes.

CILO 3

Students will discuss different writing elements and models in groups to raise awareness of and sensitivity to different writing styles in an academic context. They will learn to use the appropriate referencing system and will be tested on applying in-text citations and end-text reference lists.

Statement about Generative AI Tools use in Assessed Assignment Tasks



Given the proliferation of generative AI tools and their utility in teaching and learning, in alignment with the University’s relevant principles, the default approach is that students are empowered and guided to use generative AI tools in assessed assignment tasks. Such use must align with the University’s academic integrity guidelines for students while ensuring they are achieving the PILOs and CILOs.



In this course (Course code HDGS0306), generative AI tools can be used in the following assignment tasks in the specific way mentioned:



•	Written Assignments: Generative AI tools can be used for the following functions: 

help logically organize your points in writing;

touch up individual sentences to refine and improve your writing;

proofread your text to check for grammatical errors, typos, and misplaced or missing punctuation.
