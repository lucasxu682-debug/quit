Course Aims: 
The course aims to teach the theory and practical applications of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and machine learning. Topics include the history of AI and its current advancements, an introduction to search techniques, knowledge representation and semantic web, basic image recognition, natural language processing, robotics, symbolic artificial intelligence and machine learning, artificial neural networks with TensorFlow, cloud artificial intelligence tools and Application Program Interfaces (API). The social implications and ethical issues of AI will also be discussed.

Course Intended Learning Outcomes (CILOs)
CILOsBy the end of the course, students should be able to:CILO 1Describe the history of AI and its current developments;CILO 2Distinguish between a variety of techniques in machine learning and symbolic AI;CILO 3Apply symbolic AI and machine learning techniques to computer vision, natural language processing and robotics; andCILO 4Discuss the social implications and ethics of AI.
Teaching and Learning Activities (TLAs)
CILOsTLAs will include the following:CILOs 1-4Interactive lectures supplemented with slides, reading notes, video clips and other
supporting materials.CILOs 1-3Class discussion on the benefits and limitations of symbolic AI and machine
learning techniques applied to various areas.CILOs 1-3Computer laboratory activities:
* Demonstration of different areas in AI/ML.
* Exercises to allow students to acquire hands-on experience in AI.CILO 4Cases to illustrate the social implications and ethics of AI as well as difficulties and dilemmas encountered and their possible solutions.

Statement about Generative AI Tools use in Assessed Assignment Tasks

Given the proliferation of generative AI tools and their utility in teaching and learning,
in alignment with the University's relevant principles, the default approach is that
students are empowered and guided to use generative AI tools in assessed assignment
tasks. Such use must align with the University's academic integrity guidelines for
students while ensuring they are achieving the PILOs and CILOs.
The "Principles for the Use of Generative AI Tools in Teaching and Learning, and
Assessment" ("Principles") guides us to use generative AI tools for better learning
outcomes. You may access the "Principles" on BUniPort (U-Wide Policies & Info ->
Policies & Guidelines -> Quality Assurance, Teaching & Learning).
