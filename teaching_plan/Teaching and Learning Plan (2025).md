Course Aims: 
This course provides students with an understanding of the basic concepts and fundamental principles behind operating systems and computer networks. Topics on operating systems include CPU scheduling and processes, memory management, file systems management and I/O systems. Computer network topics include basic principles of computer networks and the internet, network topologies and technologies, network media, network protocols, IP addressing, wide area networking, and network security, management and administration.



Course Intended Learning Outcomes (CILOs)
CILOsBy the end of the course, students should be able to:CILO 1Discuss the major functions and features of operating systems;CILO 2Identify the working principles and core functions of network technologies; andCILO 3Apply appropriate operating system management tools, protocols and network services to enhance security.


Teaching and Learning Activities (TLAs)
CILOsTLAs will include the following:CILOs 1-3Lectures with presentation slides, case studies and other related learning materials about operating systems and networking.CILO 1Demonstration of different operating systems to illustrate the functions and features of operating systems.CILO 2Case sharing involving the application of various operating systems and
network technologies.
Class discussion about the adoption criteria of different operating systems
and network technologies in various scenarios.CILO 3Discussion on the advantages and concerns of management approaches for
different operating systems and the availability of network services to enhance security.
